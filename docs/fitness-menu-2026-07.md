# Fitness as a menu, 2026-07

Gabe's feedback, one idea in four answers: the fitness page was too long, and
it should work like a menu. A short, emotional front, the depth one step in.

## The structure

Three tabs under Fitness, on a section-only sub-nav (`FitnessTabs`). The
global nav is untouched, on purpose; crowding it is the failure mode Gabe
flagged himself.

- **Overview** (`/fitness`). The lean front: Gabe in first person, one action
  (Book a free consult, straight to /join), the who-this-is-for block held to
  one paragraph, and the three offer names alone as the quiet door to Offers.
  No price here, and none on the site homepage.
- **Offers** (`/fitness/offers`). The menu options: each offer with its price
  on the card, value first and the number after. The only place on the site a
  fitness price appears. Packages stay the single line "Packages available."
  until Gabe confirms real names and contents. The promotions blurb names
  nothing specific on purpose. FAQ condensed to a native accordion at the
  bottom. Ends on the consult band.
- **About Gabe** (`/fitness/about`). The story, the scope note, the six-month
  arc, and the proof block, which stays labeled placeholders until real client
  words exist. No CTA band here by design; Overview and Offers carry it.

## Open for Gabe to confirm

- Three tabs versus a fourth (How it works). Built as three, his call.
- Price only on Offers. Built that way, confirm.
- Real package names and contents to replace "Packages available."
- The Overview hero speaks as "I'm Gabe" now; swap the wording if he wants it
