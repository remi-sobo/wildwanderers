# Homepage redesign, July 2026

The brief: take the site from poetic-but-vague to instantly clear. A parent
should understand within five seconds that this is an outdoor movement and
mentorship program for boys and their dads and mentors, starting on the
Baylands, and know that the first step is joining the first circle.

## Audit: the biggest clarity problems

1. The hero positions Wild Wanderers as "wellness for the whole family."
   The boys program, the actual offer, does not appear until section four.
   A visitor cannot tell what this is, who it is for, or what to do.
2. No why-now section. The strongest emotional argument (screens, stillness,
   isolation) is compressed into two sentences in the Movement manifesto.
3. No plain what-it-is section. The confirmed facts (ages 6 to 13, groups of
   8 to 10, about one adult per six boys, Bedwell Bayfront Park, weekly by
   season) live only on the program page. Concreteness is what makes a
   premium site feel trustworthy; the homepage has almost none.
4. No boy outcomes and no clear dads-are-not-spectators message. The two
   audiences (the boy, the man) never get their own answer.
5. CTA labels are soft ("Start the conversation") and inconsistent
   ("Reach out", "Join the first circle", "Meet the boys' program").
6. Section order buries the offer: Chapters (the expansion vision) arrives
   before the visitor knows what chapter one even is.
7. The animals read as decoration, not as the field-guide system they are.
8. Meet Gabe is thin, and the footer is minimal.

## The new structure (one page, one story)

1. Hero: "Boys were born to move." Eyebrow names the program and chapter.
   Subhead says exactly what this is. Primary CTA: Join the first Baylands
   circle. Secondary: See how it works (anchor).
2. Why now: "Childhood has gotten too still." Three cards: less screen /
   more sky, less sitting / more strength, less isolation / more belonging.
3. What it is (dark): a weekly trail rhythm, said plainly, with the
   confirmed facts as a spec row and the Baylands badge.
4. How a day moves: Observe, Breathe, Explore, Reflect (kept, copy tuned).
5. The field guide: the four animals as specimen cards, with the
   memory-anchor line.
6. For boys: "Strong bodies. Soft hearts. Wide eyes." Six outcomes.
7. For dads and mentors (dark): "He does not do this alone."
8. The Baylands is Chapter One: kept, tightened, framed as the beginning
   of a movement. Horizon chapters stay aspirational, never dated.
9. Ages and stages: Notice, Practice, Belong, now with the confirmed ages.
10. Meet Gabe: "Built by a father on the trail."
11. Closing CTA: "Join the first Baylands circle." plus "Talk with Gabe."

## Guardrails held (CLAUDE.md)

- Conversation first: every primary CTA lands on /join, which is the
  inquiry conversation, not a registration. Scarcity stays in body copy.
- Only confirmed facts are published. No price, no dates, no testimonials.
- Boys focus stays framed as Gabe's calling, never as policy. Horizon
  chapters stay uncommitted. Family stays roots, not face.
- Design system unchanged: same tokens, type, ridgeline discipline, one
  rAF loop, reduced-motion gates, transform and opacity only.

## Out of scope this pass

Interior pages keep their structure (they already answer the deep
questions); only their closing CTA labels are aligned. The join form,
routes, and deploy setup are untouched.
